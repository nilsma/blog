Why You Should Vote No on Keystone XL!

by Corey Smiths - April 8th, 2014

Yet again Capitalism wants to feed on Our Earth in the name of Profit, where a few gain by sacrificing that of the many. However, the suggested TransCanada Keystone XL Pipeline has not yet been approved, pending a Presidential Permit by President Obama.

Luckily, we have big players like Tom Steyer and the NextGen Climate Action organization on our side - heavy-hitters with the sentiment to fight the environmental exploitation and with the funding to back it up: Tom Steyer is putting $100 million in the 2014 Election. Last year they fronted environmental issues by balancing the scales in various elections around the country, notably they also show willingness for going after Democrats supporting the pipeline.

According to The Washington Post the Canadians' pipeline will stretch from Alberta and The Northern Great Plains to refineries on the Gulf Coast - cutting straight through the very Heartland of America. Along its route it will threaten important natural resources such as the Ogallala Aquifer and, according to an analysis by the Center for Biological Diversity, it will threaten a wide range of endangered birds and animals.

The pipeline will potentially bring a few new jobs - an estimated 50 new jobs for people in the regions, which is about the same as a smaller mall would employ - but are we really willing to gamble with our drinking water and the saftey of our children?

As the video clearly shows there has been a lot of pipeline-spils, and there are no reasons why we should not expect there to be more, and why we should not fight the pipeline at all costs. Here is a little list of a few of the problems since 1986:

  • There has been 7978 incidents
  • There has been 512 fatalities
  • There has been 2360 injuries
  • There has been almost $7,000,000,000 of property damage

"The heads of these companies don’t have to live with their mistakes."
Kate Comegys,
Ex Wall Street employee

Clearly, there is no reason not to anticipate oilspills along the Keystone pipeline, the only questions are how often they will come, where will they occur, and how much damage will they inflict on our environment, our water, our children and our future. And with 830 000 barrels squeezed through the pipes each day, you just know that the impact will be devastating when it happens.

In the words of Kate Comegys, who used to work on Wall Street: "The heads of these companies don’t have to live with their mistakes." - but the rest of us will while the oil-brass are living it up some place else on the globe where the water is clean, their childrens future are safe, and there isn't an oil pipeline in miles sight in any direction.
